<p>Gamification has become an increasingly popular strategy for promoting healthy behaviors from medication adherence to chronic condition management.</p><p>A new <a href="https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamanetworkopen/fullarticle/2780065">study published in JAMA Network</a> examined the effectiveness of gamification in promoting physical activity and weight loss among adults with uncontrolled Type 2 diabetes.</p><p>"Gamification is commonly used in wellness programs and smartphone apps, but often is not designed to incorporate insights on how people behave and have not been well-tested over longer periods," Dr. Mitesh Patel, the director of Penn Medicine's Nudge Unit and the lead author of the study, said in a statement.&nbsp;</p><p>The one-year intervention gave participants a wearable, electronic step counter and an electronic scale, both from Withings, and randomly separated them into four arms â€“ one control group and three gamification groups.</p>
